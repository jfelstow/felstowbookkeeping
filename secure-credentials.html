For My Clients

How to securely send me your passwords.

Email and text are not safe places for passwords — both leave permanent copies that can be searched, screenshotted, or breached later. Instead, I use a free tool called Bitwarden Send that lets you share a password through an encrypted link that self-destructs after I open it.

No account needed on your end. Takes about 30 seconds.

AThe 30-second version

1

Go to Bitwarden Send

Open the link below. You don't need to create an account or sign in — you can use it right away.

Open Bitwarden Send →
2

Paste your password(s) into the text box

If you have multiple credentials to send, just put them all in one Send and label each one. Example:

Chase Business Checking
Login: jane@mybusiness.com
Password: ExamplePass123!

Amex Business
Login: jane@mybusiness.com
Password: Different456@

That's it — one Send can hold everything I need.

3

Set the expiration & access limit

Under "Options," set:

  • Deletion date: 1 or 2 days from now
  • Maximum access count: 1 (the link self-destructs after I open it)

These are the defaults Bitwarden recommends — they protect you even if the link is forwarded or intercepted.

4

Click "Save" and copy the link

Bitwarden gives you a long URL. Send only that link to me — via email, text, or however we usually communicate. Without the link, the contents can't be read; even Bitwarden themselves can't see what you sent.

Send to: jake.felstow@gmail.com · or text 586-713-7783

5

I'll confirm receipt

Once I open the Send, the contents auto-delete from Bitwarden's servers. I'll reply to confirm I got it and have the credentials safely stored in my own password vault.

BQuick reference

✓ Do
  • Use Bitwarden Send for any login I ask for
  • Bundle multiple credentials in one Send
  • Set expiration to 1–2 days, 1 access
  • Send the link via email, text, or call
✗ Don't
  • Email me a password as plain text
  • Text me a password as plain text
  • Put a password in the onboarding form
  • Worry — if you do it wrong, I'll just ask you to resend it

CAlternatives if Bitwarden Send isn't an option

Phone call. Just call me at 586-713-7783 and read it to me — I'll type it directly into my password manager. Takes a minute.

In person. If we're meeting, I can input it on my computer while you watch.

1Password share link. If you already use 1Password, that works perfectly too — just send me the share link the same way.

DWhy this matters

Your bookkeeping involves the keys to your financial life — bank logins, payroll, QuickBooks. The single biggest risk in modern small-business security isn't fancy hacks; it's credentials sitting in old emails and text threads that get breached years later.

Bitwarden Send removes that risk entirely. The contents are encrypted before they leave your browser, can only be opened with the unique link, and disappear permanently after I open them. There's nothing left behind to leak.

Bitwarden is the same open-source password tool used by individuals, small businesses, and Fortune 500 companies. It's free for what we're using it for.

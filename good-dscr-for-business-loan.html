What is a good DSCR for a business loan?

By Felstow Bookkeeping & Consulting · Updated July 2026

If a lender declines your application, the odds are the decision came down to one number: the debt-service coverage ratio. Here is what counts as a good DSCR, how underwriters compute it, and what to do when yours is short.

The formula

DSCR = cash flow available for debt service ÷ total debt service. In its most common small-business form: your average monthly net income, plus allowable add-backs (depreciation, amortization, interest already expensed, documented one-time costs), divided by the sum of every monthly debt payment — including the loan you are applying for.

A DSCR of 1.0× means cash flow exactly covers the payments, with nothing to spare. Anything below 1.0× means the business cannot make the payment from operations at all.

The benchmarks lenders actually use

DSCRHow lenders read it
Below 1.0×Cash flow doesn't cover debt payments. Decline, absent strong collateral or guarantor income.
1.0× – 1.15×Technically covered, no margin for a slow month. Most banks pass.
1.15× – 1.25×The gray zone. SBA lending commonly requires at least 1.15×; conventional banks usually want more.
1.25× – 1.5×The standard bar. 1.25× is the figure most commercial lenders quote as their minimum for approval.
Above 1.5×Strong. Expect better pricing and more lender competition for the deal.

Two nuances worth knowing. First, many banks compute a global DSCR that pools the owner's personal income and personal debt with the business's — a strong salary can rescue a thin business ratio, and heavy personal debt can sink a good one. Second, the ratio is usually computed on your worst recent year, not your best forecast.

Add-backs: the part borrowers under-claim

Underwriters don't use raw net income; they use cash flow. Non-cash and non-recurring expenses get added back: depreciation and amortization always, interest on debts being refinanced, and genuinely one-time costs if you can document them. On a business netting $80,000 with $20,000 of depreciation, claiming the add-back moves DSCR by 25% — often the whole distance between decline and approval. Unclaimed add-backs are the most common self-inflicted wound in small-business applications.

Five ways to raise a short DSCR

  1. Lengthen the term. $100,000 at 9.5% costs about $2,097/month over 5 years but $1,294/month over 10 — the same loan, a dramatically better ratio.
  2. Borrow less. Underwriters respect a right-sized ask; it signals you did this math yourself.
  3. Refinance existing debt into the new loan. Consolidating a high-payment note shrinks the denominator.
  4. Document every add-back. Depreciation, amortization, true one-timers — with evidence.
  5. Wait one strong quarter. If revenue is trending up, three more months of data can lift the trailing-twelve-month average enough to clear the bar.

Frequently asked questions

What DSCR does the SBA require?

SBA 7(a) underwriting standards commonly require a minimum DSCR of 1.15× on the subject business, though individual lenders frequently hold to their own higher floor of 1.25×. The SBA guarantee makes lenders more flexible than on conventional loans, not unconditionally flexible.

Is DSCR calculated monthly or annually?

Either — the ratio is the same math at both scales. Small-business lenders typically work from annual or trailing-twelve-month figures; monthly framing (average monthly cash flow ÷ total monthly payments) is identical and easier to sanity-check.

Does personal income count toward DSCR?

Often, yes. Many banks compute a "global" DSCR combining business cash flow with the guarantor's personal income and personal debt payments. If your business ratio is thin but you have strong outside income, ask whether the lender underwrites globally.

What is a good DSCR for a rental property or DSCR loan?

Real-estate DSCR loans use the same math with rental income as the cash flow. Most DSCR mortgage products want at least 1.0×–1.25×, with the best pricing above 1.25×. This article focuses on operating-business loans, where 1.25× is the standard bar.

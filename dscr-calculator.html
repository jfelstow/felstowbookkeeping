How this calculator works

The debt-service coverage ratio compares the cash a business generates with the debt payments it has to make:

DSCR = (average monthly net income + add-backs) ÷ total monthly debt payments, including the proposed loan.

The proposed payment is computed as a standard fully-amortized loan — the same math your bank uses. Add-backs matter more than most borrowers realize: depreciation and amortization are accounting expenses, not cash leaving the business, so underwriters add them back to income before judging coverage. Skipping them understates your own ratio, sometimes by the entire margin between decline and approval — here's the full guide to how lenders judge DSCR.

Frequently asked questions

How do you calculate DSCR?

Cash flow available for debt service divided by total debt service. For a small business: average monthly net income plus add-backs (depreciation, amortization, interest being refinanced, documented one-time costs), divided by all monthly debt payments including the proposed new loan.

What is a good DSCR?

Most commercial lenders want at least 1.25×. SBA 7(a) underwriting commonly requires a minimum of 1.15×. Below 1.0× means cash flow doesn't cover debt payments, and most applications are declined.

Should I use net income or EBITDA?

Small-business lenders typically start from net income and add back depreciation, amortization, and interest being refinanced — which lands close to EBITDA. This calculator's add-backs field does exactly that. Whichever you use, be consistent and able to document it.

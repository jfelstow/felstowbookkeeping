SBA 7(a) loan requirements: what you actually need to qualify

By Felstow Bookkeeping & Consulting · Updated July 2026

The SBA 7(a) is the workhorse of American small-business lending — hundreds of thousands of loans a year, terms up to 10–25 years, and rates conventional lenders rarely match. The trade-off is paperwork and rules. Here is the honest version of what it takes.

Baseline eligibility

  • For-profit business operating in the U.S. — nonprofits and passive investment vehicles are out.
  • Meets SBA size standards — generous by design; the large majority of American small businesses qualify on size.
  • Owner equity and character — owners of 20%+ personally guarantee the loan, and the application covers citizenship/residency status and criminal history.
  • "Credit elsewhere" test — technically, you shouldn't be able to get the same credit on reasonable terms without the guarantee. In practice lenders handle this; you just tell the truth on the forms.

The numbers that get underwritten

  • Credit score. Smaller 7(a) loans are pre-screened with the SBA's SBSS score (a blend of personal and business credit); the commonly cited minimum is around 155 out of 300, and most funded borrowers have personal FICO scores of roughly 650+.
  • Debt-service coverage. SBA standards commonly require at least a 1.15× DSCR, and many participating lenders hold their usual 1.25× line anyway. This is the ratio to know cold — here's how lenders judge it, and you can compute yours in 30 seconds.
  • Time in business. Two-plus years makes everything easier. Startups can qualify under 7(a), but expect projections, industry experience, and a real equity injection to carry the file.
  • Collateral. The SBA does not decline solely for insufficient collateral, but lenders take what's available — business assets first, and liens on personal real estate can enter the picture on larger loans.

The document stack

Everything in the standard business-loan checklist — P&L, balance sheet, business and personal tax returns, bank statements, debt schedule — plus the SBA layer: Form 1919 (borrower information), a personal financial statement, articles of organization, licenses, your lease, and for many uses of funds a business plan with projections.

What actually disqualifies applicants

  1. DSCR below the line. The most common substantive decline. Fix the ratio (smaller ask, longer term, documented add-backs) before applying, not after.
  2. Books that don't match tax returns. Unexplained gaps read as either sloppiness or fiction; both are fatal.
  3. Delinquent government debt. Defaulted federal student loans, unresolved tax liens, or prior SBA losses are hard stops.
  4. Recent personal credit events without a clean written explanation.
  5. Ineligible use of funds — e.g., passive real-estate investment doesn't fit 7(a).

Timeline

From complete application to funding, plan on several weeks to a couple of months depending on the lender and program; SBA Express variants trade speed for smaller maximums. The single biggest thing you control is submitting a complete, reconciled financial package on day one — incomplete files sit in queues, complete ones move. Frequently asked questions

What credit score do you need for an SBA loan?

For smaller 7(a) loans, the SBA's SBSS pre-screen commonly uses a minimum around 155 (on a 0–300 scale), which blends personal and business credit. As a practical matter, most approved borrowers have personal FICO scores of roughly 650 or better; stronger scores mean better odds and pricing.

What DSCR does an SBA 7(a) loan require?

SBA underwriting standards commonly cite a minimum debt-service coverage ratio of 1.15×, though many participating lenders apply their own 1.25× floor. Compute yours before applying — it is the number the decision most often turns on.

Can a startup get an SBA 7(a) loan?

Yes — 7(a) funds startups every year, but the bar is higher: expect to show relevant industry experience, a meaningful owner cash injection (often 10%+ of the project), a credible business plan with projections, and strong personal credit.

How long does SBA approval take?

Typically several weeks to a couple of months from complete application to funding, varying by lender, loan size, and program. Submitting a complete, reconciled financial package up front is the biggest accelerator available to the borrower.

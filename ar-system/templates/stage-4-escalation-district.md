# Stage 4 — Escalation to business office (due + 35 days, district)

Districts rarely refuse to pay — a 35-days-late district invoice is almost always stuck in
process (unmatched PO, unconfirmed receiving, wrong remit info, missing vendor form).
This stage is a PHONE CALL to the AP supervisor or district business manager, plus the
paper-trail email below cc'ing the original AP contact. Attach the invoice and statement.

## Call goals
- Identify the specific process failure holding payment.
- Get a named person who owns the fix and a check-run date.
- Stay warm: this relationship feeds the fall/spring order cycle.

## Follow-up email

Subject: Escalation: invoice {{invoice_number}} / PO {{po_number}} — {{days_past_due}} days past due

Hello {{escalation_contact_name}},

Thank you for your time today. Summarizing where things stand: invoice
{{invoice_number}} for {{amount}} against PO {{po_number}} was due {{due_date}} and is now
{{days_past_due}} days past due. We've confirmed delivery and have followed up with your
AP office on {{touch_dates}} without resolution.

As discussed: {{commitment_summary}}.

Invoice and statement of account are attached. If anything further is needed from us to
complete processing, I'll turn it around same day.

We appreciate the district's business and want to keep {{business_name}}'s deliveries
running smoothly this year.

{{signature}}
